advocacy

let's advocate together

As a queer woman of color, my identity remains as the venerable and vulnerable reminders of who I am, how far I have become, and who I will become. advocating for the queer community has not only been a personal responsibility of mine, but given me alot of pride. below are a few work of social justice campaign and articles that has been pivotal to my advocacy for the queer community

luckyxlove article

As a hopeful designer interested in all things products and the digital design landscape, representation and visibility within the products is incredibly important to me. The importance of visibility should not only be practiced on shelves and in screen, but also behind and that means creation and development of designs from those that are usually underrepresented. In reflection to the luckyxlove campaign, I wrote an article for GLAAD's amp channel reflecting upon my design experience and the confidence designing a shirt for lucky brand has given be as a queer person

conversation for change podcast

Establishing a safe and inclusive university environment and an LGBTQ+ inclusive curriculum is vital to ensuring that LGBTQ+ students on campus feel seen and heard. This podcast discusses the importance of an LGBTQ+ inclusive environment and advice for queer students on campus on how to navigate the university no matter your sexual and gender identity

how alan turing inspires me article

I'm very interested in the field of human-computer interaction and the design space, Alan Turing has inspired me in my space within technology as a queer woman. I talk about how his story of resilience and the legacy he has left behind in our world has inspired me to continue this pursuit and how I can strive to continue making a social impact using my passion for design

glaad x teenvogue

I was super honored and privileged to be listed under GLAADXTeenVogue's inaugural 20Under20 LGBTQ+ activist that highlights members of the new generation of LGBTQ voices who are using their platform to advance acceptance, equality & visibility for LGBTQ people and working to shape the future of media and activism and who demand to be seen and heard

how I pursue tech as a queer woman of color article

In this article for GLAAD's amp channel, I provide my narrative on my experience pursuing tech thus far, and how I navigate through the uncertainty and the challenges of being queer in tech

spirit day 2020 campaign

For GLAAD's 2020 Spirit Day Against LGBTQ+ Youth bullying, I created digital website campaign fully funded by Squarespace thanks to their Make My Space contest that serves as an interactive, informative, and creative means of advocating and make a statement against LGBTQ+ youth bullying. The campaign allows for LGBTQ+ folks and allies to speak out by contributing towards our online mural and gallery in purple spirit. LGBTQ+ youth deserve to be loved, to be accepted, and be surrounded by a community of enduring support for them to live and love as who they are, so no LGBT youth is left behind

spirit day 2019 contribution

For GLAAD's 2019 Spirit Day Against LGBTQ+ Youth bullying, I contributed a bit of my voice to the importance for educators to ensure and provide a safe & accepting environment for LGBT youth for them to succeed and grow

nyc 50th world pride march

For my second pride march, I had an amazing time marching with GLAAD for the 50th anniversary of pride back home in NYC. It was an incredibly welcoming and exciting time for me to be in an accepting space of not only celebrating the strides we made, but most importantly, remembering that pride is a protest. We must honor the folks and continue to work & uplift marginalzied communities. The work that trans activists have done to create a space for queer workers of all industries, queer youth of color, queer activists, homeless queer folks, and every queer person in between will not be forgotten. Trans activist Sylvia’s legacy and the intersectionality of Marsha’s black transgender experience - for example - must be remembered, shared, amplified, and honored. Centering our work around trans rights gives trans folks the space to take back the radical movement they started. There is no queer liberation without trans liberation. NYC Pride is a reminder of the long road we have ahead of us and the work we have to do to ensure LGBTQ+ acceptance all around
